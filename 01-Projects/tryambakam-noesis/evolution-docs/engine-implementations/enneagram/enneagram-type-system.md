# Enneagram Type System - Complete 9 Types

## Type 1: The Perfectionist

**Alternative Names:** The Reformer, The Idealist  
**Center:** Body (Instinctive)  
**Core Emotion:** Anger/Resentment

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To be good, right, perfect, and to improve everything
- **Core Fear:** Being corrupt, evil, or defective
- **Core Desire:** To be good, to have integrity, to be balanced
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you do what is right"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Anger
- **Virtue:** Serenity
- **Passion:** Resentment
- **Fixation:** Perfectionism
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Perfection
- **Trap:** Perfection (seeking the unattainable)

### Keywords
perfectionist, principled, reformer, critical, idealistic, responsible, orderly, rigid

---

## Type 2: The Helper

**Alternative Names:** The Giver, The People Pleaser  
**Center:** Heart (Emotional)  
**Core Emotion:** Shame

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To feel loved and needed and to express their feelings for others
- **Core Fear:** Being unloved or unwanted for themselves alone
- **Core Desire:** To feel loved
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you are loved by others"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Pride
- **Virtue:** Humility
- **Passion:** Flattery/Pride
- **Fixation:** Freedom (from need)
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Will/Freedom
- **Trap:** Helping (compulsive giving to get love)

### Keywords
helper, caring, generous, possessive, manipulative, people-pleasing, warm, intrusive

---

## Type 3: The Achiever

**Alternative Names:** The Performer, The Succeeder  
**Center:** Heart (Emotional)  
**Core Emotion:** Shame

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To feel valuable and worthwhile, to be affirmed, to distinguish themselves from others
- **Core Fear:** Being worthless or without value apart from their achievements
- **Core Desire:** To feel valuable and worthwhile
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you are successful and others think well of you"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Deceit
- **Virtue:** Truthfulness/Authenticity
- **Passion:** Vanity
- **Fixation:** Vanity/Deceit
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Hope/Law
- **Trap:** Efficiency (mistaking doing for being)

### Keywords
achiever, ambitious, image-conscious, competent, driven, status-seeking, efficient, workaholic

---

## Type 4: The Individualist

**Alternative Names:** The Romantic, The Artist  
**Center:** Heart (Emotional)  
**Core Emotion:** Shame

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To find themselves and their significance, to create an identity from their inner experience
- **Core Fear:** Having no identity or personal significance
- **Core Desire:** To find themselves and their significance
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you are true to yourself"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Envy
- **Virtue:** Equanimity/Emotional Balance
- **Passion:** Melancholy/Envy
- **Fixation:** Melancholy
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Origin
- **Trap:** Authenticity (over-identifying with emotional states)

### Keywords
individualist, creative, sensitive, dramatic, self-absorbed, melancholic, unique, emotional

---

## Type 5: The Investigator

**Alternative Names:** The Observer, The Thinker  
**Center:** Head (Mental)  
**Core Emotion:** Fear

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To be capable and competent, to understand the environment, to have everything figured out
- **Core Fear:** Being useless, helpless, or incapable
- **Core Desire:** To be capable and competent
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you are knowledgeable and competent"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Avarice (hoarding time, energy, knowledge)
- **Virtue:** Non-attachment/Generosity
- **Passion:** Stinginess/Avarice
- **Fixation:** Stinginess
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Omniscience/Transparency
- **Trap:** Observer (detached observation instead of participation)

### Keywords
investigator, perceptive, isolated, detached, cerebral, minimalist, analytical, withdrawn

---

## Type 6: The Loyalist

**Alternative Names:** The Skeptic, The Questioner  
**Center:** Head (Mental)  
**Core Emotion:** Fear

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To have security and support, to have guidance, to test the attitudes of others
- **Core Fear:** Being without support or guidance
- **Core Desire:** To have security and support
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you do what is expected of you"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Fear
- **Virtue:** Courage
- **Passion:** Fear/Anxiety
- **Fixation:** Cowardice/Doubt
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Faith/Strength
- **Trap:** Security (external rather than internal)

### Keywords
loyalist, committed, security-oriented, anxious, suspicious, responsible, cautious, indecisive

---

## Type 7: The Enthusiast

**Alternative Names:** The Epicure, The Optimist  
**Center:** Head (Mental)  
**Core Emotion:** Fear (escaped through activity)

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To maintain their happiness and keep themselves occupied and engaged
- **Core Fear:** Being trapped, deprived, or in pain
- **Core Desire:** To maintain their happiness and satisfaction
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you get what you need"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Gluttony (insatiable hunger for experience)
- **Virtue:** Sobriety (being present with reality)
- **Passion:** Planning/Gluttony
- **Fixation:** Planning
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Wisdom/Work/Plan
- **Trap:** Idealism (positive reframing to avoid pain)

### Keywords
enthusiast, spontaneous, versatile, scattered, acquisitive, optimistic, stimulation-seeking, escapist

---

## Type 8: The Challenger

**Alternative Names:** The Leader, The Boss  
**Center:** Body (Instinctive)  
**Core Emotion:** Anger/Rage (expressed directly)

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To be self-reliant and in control of their own life and destiny
- **Core Fear:** Being controlled or vulnerable to others
- **Core Desire:** To be self-reliant and in control
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if you are strong and in control"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Lust (for intensity, excess, control)
- **Virtue:** Innocence (simplicity, openness, vulnerability)
- **Passion:** Vengeance/Lust
- **Fixation:** Vengeance
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Truth
- **Trap:** Justice (taking matters into own hands)

### Keywords
challenger, powerful, dominating, confrontational, protective, assertive, intense, controlling

---

## Type 9: The Peacemaker

**Alternative Names:** The Mediator, The Harmonizer  
**Center:** Body (Instinctive)  
**Core Emotion:** Anger (numbed/repressed)

### Core Dynamics

- **Core Motivation:** To maintain their inner and outer peace and to avoid conflict
- **Core Fear:** Loss of connection and fragmentation of their world
- **Core Desire:** To have inner and outer peace
- **Basic Proposition:** "You are good or okay if those around you are good or okay"

### Traditional Elements

- **Vice:** Sloth (spiritual laziness, self-forgetting)
- **Virtue:** Right Action (decisive engagement)
- **Passion:** Indolence/Sloth
- **Fixation:** Indolence
- **Holy Idea:** Holy Love
- **Trap:** Seeking (looking outside for what's already present)

### Keywords
peacemaker, receptive, reassuring, complacent, resigned, accommodating, comfortable, stubborn

## Type Recognition Patterns

### Body Types (8, 9, 1)
**Shared Traits:**
- Instinctive, gut-level responses
- Issues with anger and boundaries
- Focus on control and autonomy
- Physical presence awareness

**Distinctions:**
- **Type 8:** Expresses anger directly, confrontational
- **Type 9:** Numbs anger, conflict-avoidant
- **Type 1:** Represses anger, becomes resentment

### Heart Types (2, 3, 4)
**Shared Traits:**
- Emotional, feeling-centered
- Issues with shame and image
- Focus on identity and worth
- Relationship-oriented

**Distinctions:**
- **Type 2:** Worth through being needed
- **Type 3:** Worth through achievement
- **Type 4:** Worth through uniqueness

### Head Types (5, 6, 7)
**Shared Traits:**
- Mental, thinking-centered
- Issues with fear and anxiety
- Focus on security and planning
- Future-oriented

**Distinctions:**
- **Type 5:** Withdraws from fear
- **Type 6:** Projects fear outward
- **Type 7:** Escapes fear through activity

## Growth Recommendations by Type

### Type 1
1. Practice accepting "good enough" rather than perfect
2. Learn to express anger directly rather than through criticism
3. Develop spontaneity and playfulness
4. Practice self-compassion and forgiveness
5. Embrace the beauty of imperfection

### Type 2
1. Develop awareness of own needs and feelings
2. Practice receiving without needing to give back
3. Set healthy boundaries in relationships
4. Acknowledge and work with pride patterns
5. Learn to value self apart from others' approval

### Type 3
1. Slow down and be present with feelings
2. Practice being vs. doing
3. Develop authentic self beneath image
4. Allow vulnerability and "failure"
5. Value self apart from achievements

### Type 4
1. Move from envying to appreciating
2. Practice being ordinary
3. Develop discipline and follow-through
4. Balance feeling with action
5. Recognize abundance rather than lack

### Type 5
1. Engage with life rather than observe
2. Share knowledge and resources generously
3. Connect with body and emotions
4. Practice participation over preparation
5. Develop trust in own capability

### Type 6
1. Trust own inner knowing
2. Move from doubt to faith
3. Practice decisive action
4. Develop self-confidence
5. Distinguish real from projected danger

### Type 7
1. Stay present with difficult feelings
2. Practice depth over breadth
3. Develop discipline and commitment
4. Allow full experience of current moment
5. Transform gluttony to satisfaction

### Type 8
1. Develop vulnerability and softness
2. Practice listening and receptivity
3. Recognize impact on others
4. Allow weakness and need
5. Transform lust to innocence

### Type 9
1. Assert own position and opinions
2. Practice healthy conflict engagement
3. Develop priorities and say no
4. Stay awake to own anger
5. Transform sloth to right action

## Holy Ideas

Each type has access to a transcendent truth:

1. **Holy Perfection** - All is already perfect as it is
2. **Holy Will/Freedom** - Divine will flows through all, no separation
3. **Holy Hope/Law** - All beings have intrinsic value beyond doing
4. **Holy Origin** - All arise from same source, no separation
5. **Holy Omniscience** - All is known, nothing hidden
6. **Holy Faith** - Reality is trustworthy and benevolent
7. **Holy Wisdom** - Reality is perfect as unfolding
8. **Holy Truth** - What is, is; no need for force
9. **Holy Love** - All is one, separation is illusion
