## Quick Reference

### The Nine Types

| Type | Name | Center | Core Motivation | Core Fear |
|------|------|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| 1 | The Perfectionist | Body | To be right, good, perfect | Being corrupt, evil, defective |
| 2 | The Helper | Heart | To be loved and needed | Being unloved or unwanted |
| 3 | The Achiever | Heart | To be valuable and successful | Being worthless or failing |
| 4 | The Individualist | Heart | To be unique and authentic | Having no identity or significance |
| 5 | The Investigator | Head | To be competent and knowledgeable | Being useless or incapable |
| 6 | The Loyalist | Head | To be secure and supported | Being without support or guidance |
| 7 | The Enthusiast | Head | To be satisfied and content | Being deprived or trapped in pain |
| 8 | The Challenger | Body | To be strong and protect self | Being controlled or harmed by others |
| 9 | The Peacemaker | Body | To have peace and harmony | Conflict and disconnection |

### Three Centers of Intelligence

1. **Body Center (Types 8, 9, 1)** - Instinctive, gut-level intelligence focused on boundaries and action
   - Core Emotion: Anger/Rage
   - Focus: Power, autonomy, and physical presence

2. **Heart Center (Types 2, 3, 4)** - Emotional intelligence focused on identity and relationships
   - Core Emotion: Shame
   - Focus: Image, connection, and being valued

3. **Head Center (Types 5, 6, 7)** - Mental intelligence focused on security and planning
   - Core Emotion: Fear
   - Focus: Safety, thinking, and future orientation

### Wings (Adjacent Type Influences)

Each type has two wings - the types on either side:
- Type 1: Wings 9 or 2 (1w9 or 1w2)
- Type 2: Wings 1 or 3 (2w1 or 2w3)
- Type 3: Wings 2 or 4 (3w2 or 3w4)
- And so on...

One wing typically dominates, adding flavor to the core type.

### Arrows (Integration/Disintegration)

**Integration Paths (Growth Direction - Healthy Stress):**
- 1 → 7 (Perfectionist becomes spontaneous)
- 2 → 4 (Helper becomes self-aware)
- 3 → 6 (Achiever becomes committed)
- 4 → 1 (Individualist becomes principled)
- 5 → 8 (Investigator becomes confident)
- 6 → 9 (Loyalist becomes peaceful)
- 7 → 5 (Enthusiast becomes focused)
- 8 → 2 (Challenger becomes caring)
- 9 → 3 (Peacemaker becomes active)

**Disintegration Paths (Unhealthy Stress):**
- 1 → 4 (becomes moody, self-pitying)
- 2 → 8 (becomes aggressive, demanding)
- 3 → 9 (becomes complacent, disengaged)
- 4 → 2 (becomes clingy, manipulative)
- 5 → 7 (becomes scattered, impulsive)
- 6 → 3 (becomes competitive, workaholic)
- 7 → 1 (becomes critical, perfectionistic)
- 8 → 5 (becomes withdrawn, isolated)
- 9 → 6 (becomes anxious, worrying)

### Instinctual Variants (Subtypes)

Each type expresses through three instinctual drives:

1. **Self-Preservation (SP)** - Focus on physical safety, security, comfort
2. **Social (SO)** - Focus on group dynamics, belonging, contribution
3. **Sexual (SX)** - Focus on one-to-one intensity, attraction, energy transmission

One instinct dominates, creating 27 subtypes (9 types × 3 variants).

### Levels of Development

Nine levels from healthy to unhealthy:

**Healthy (1-3):**
- Level 1: Liberation - Operating from essence
- Level 2: Psychological Capacity - Healthy ego functioning
- Level 3: Social Value - Contributing meaningfully

**Average (4-6):**
- Level 4: Imbalance - Type fixation begins
- Level 5: Interpersonal Control - Manipulating to get needs met
- Level 6: Overcompensation - Rigid patterns emerge

**Unhealthy (7-9):**
- Level 7: Violation - Harming self and others
- Level 8: Obsession and Compulsion - Losing control
- Level 9: Pathological Destructiveness - Complete breakdown
