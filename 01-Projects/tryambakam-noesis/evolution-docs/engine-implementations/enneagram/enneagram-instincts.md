# Enneagram Instinctual Variants - The Three Instincts

## Overview

The three instinctual variants (also called subtypes) represent basic survival drives that combine with the nine types to create 27 distinct expressions (9 types Ã— 3 variants). These instincts are pre-Enneagram - they're biological drives present in all humans.

**Key Principle:** Your dominant instinct shapes how your type expresses itself in the world.

---

## The Three Instincts

### Self-Preservation (SP)
**Focus:** Physical safety, security, comfort, and resources  
**Domain:** Personal survival and well-being  
**Attention On:** Food, shelter, health, money, comfort, temperature

**Characteristics:**
- Practical and grounded
- Resource-conscious
- Home-focused
- Risk-averse
- Comfort-seeking
- Planning for security

**Questions:**
- "Am I safe?"
- "Do I have enough?"
- "Am I comfortable?"
- "Are my needs met?"

**Behavioral Signs:**
- Attention to physical environment
- Concern about health
- Financial planning
- Home nesting
- Resource management

---

### Social (SO)
**Focus:** Group belonging, status, and contribution  
**Domain:** Social survival and group dynamics  
**Attention On:** Hierarchy, status, belonging, recognition, group welfare

**Characteristics:**
- Group-oriented
- Status-aware
- Contribution-focused
- Networking
- Recognition-seeking
- Community-minded

**Questions:**
- "Do I belong?"
- "What's my position?"
- "Am I contributing?"
- "How am I perceived?"

**Behavioral Signs:**
- Tracking group dynamics
- Status awareness
- Large social networks
- Group involvement
- Social responsibility

---

### Sexual/One-to-One (SX)
**Focus:** Intense connection, attraction, and energy transmission  
**Domain:** One-to-one bonding and intensity  
**Attention On:** Chemistry, intensity, attraction, merger, transmission

**Characteristics:**
- Intensity-seeking
- Magnetic presence
- One-to-one focus
- Attraction-oriented
- Transmitting energy
- Edge-seeking

**Questions:**
- "Is there chemistry?"
- "Do I have their attention?"
- "Is this intense enough?"
- "Are we connected?"

**Behavioral Signs:**
- Intense eye contact
- Focus on specific individuals
- Magnetic/charismatic energy
- Pursuit of intensity
- Few but deep connections

## Instinctual Stacking

### Primary Instinct
Your **dominant** instinct - where your attention goes automatically. This is your default mode and primary concern.

### Secondary Instinct
Your **supporting** instinct - accessible but not automatic. You can use this when needed.

### Blind Spot
Your **repressed** instinct - least developed, creates vulnerability. This is where you're "blind."

### Stacking Notation

Six possible stackings:
1. **SP/SO** - Self-preservation dominant, Social secondary
2. **SP/SX** - Self-preservation dominant, Sexual secondary  
3. **SO/SP** - Social dominant, Self-preservation secondary
4. **SO/SX** - Social dominant, Sexual secondary
5. **SX/SP** - Sexual dominant, Self-preservation secondary
6. **SX/SO** - Sexual dominant, Social secondary

**Note:** The blind spot instinct is implied (whichever is missing).

## Instinct Recognition

### Self-Preservation Dominant
**You might be SP if:**
- First notice: Temperature, hunger, comfort
- Concerned about: Money, health, home
- Spend time: Nesting, planning security
- Relationships: Practical partnership
- Stressed by: Resource scarcity, discomfort

### Social Dominant
**You might be SO if:**
- First notice: Group dynamics, hierarchy
- Concerned about: Belonging, status, contribution
- Spend time: Networking, group activities
- Relationships: Positioned in network
- Stressed by: Exclusion, low status

### Sexual Dominant
**You might be SX if:**
- First notice: Chemistry, attraction, intensity
- Concerned about: Connection depth, energy
- Spend time: One-to-one interactions
- Relationships: Intense merger
- Stressed by: Lack of intensity, superficiality

## Instinctual Stacking Effects

### SP Blind (SO/SX or SX/SO)
**Blind Spot:**
- Neglects physical needs
- Poor resource management
- Health problems from neglect
- Impractical lifestyle

**Growth:** Develop practical self-care, financial planning, health awareness.

---

### SO Blind (SP/SX or SX/SP)
**Blind Spot:**
- Misses group dynamics
- Unaware of status/hierarchy
- Social faux pas
- Limited network

**Growth:** Develop social awareness, group contribution, networking skills.

---

### SX Blind (SP/SO or SO/SP)
**Blind Spot:**
- Misses chemistry/attraction
- Superficial connections
- Avoids intensity
- Uncomfortable with intimacy

**Growth:** Develop capacity for intimacy, intensity tolerance, magnetic presence.

## Countertype Variants

Certain instinct-type combinations look **different** from the typical type:

**SP Twos:** Most withdrawn Two (not typical Helper)  
**SO Sixes:** Most dutiful, intellectual Six  
**SX Sixes:** Counterphobic, aggressive (looks like Eight)  
**SO Sevens:** Self-sacrificing (not typical Enthusiast)  
**SP Fours:** Stoic, enduring (not typical Romantic)

## Working with Instincts

### Developing Dominant Instinct Consciously
1. Notice where attention goes automatically
2. See the compulsive quality
3. Witness the instinct operating
4. Choose conscious engagement
5. Transform drive to wisdom

### Developing Secondary Instinct
1. Recognize it's available but not automatic
2. Practice accessing intentionally
3. Strengthen through use
4. Balance with dominant

### Addressing Blind Spot
1. Identify which instinct you neglect
2. Notice vulnerability it creates
3. Consciously develop this area
4. Ask for support from those strong here
5. Practice new skills

## Instinct-Type Integration

### Full Identification Pattern

**Complete Formula:**
- **Core Type** (1-9)
- **Wing** (w9 or w2, etc.)
- **Instinctual Stacking** (SP/SO, SX/SP, etc.)
- **Arrows** (integration/disintegration)

## Witness Development Through Instincts

### Stage 1: Automatic Operation
- Instinct runs unconsciously
- Complete identification
- "This is just how I am"

### Stage 2: Recognition
- "I notice my SP/SO/SX focus"
- See instinctual patterns
- Beginning awareness

### Stage 3: Conscious Use
- "I can choose to engage this instinct or not"
- Intentional instinct access
- Freedom emerging

### Stage 4: Liberation
- All instincts available as tools
- No compulsive identification
- Fluid response to life

### For SO Dominant

- Challenge: Let go of status concerns

### For SX Dominant
- Practice: Practical tasks, group participation, moderation

## Summary

Instinctual variants reveal:
- **Survival strategy** (SP, SO, or SX)
- **Attention focus** (where energy goes automatically)
- **Blind spot** (neglected area creating vulnerability)
- **Subtype expression** (27 distinct type-instinct combinations)
- **Liberation path** (from compulsion to conscious choice)

**Key Teaching:** Your instinct is not your type, but it dramatically shapes how your type expresses. Witness the instinct to free its energy for conscious use.
