# Short-Form Hook Generator: Tryambakam Noesis
## 15-Second Viral Hooks for TikTok/Reels/Shorts

---

## Overview

These hooks are designed for the first 15 seconds of short-form video content. Each hook includes:
- **The spoken line** (under 5 seconds when spoken)
- **Visual direction** (the pattern interrupt)
- **Script continuation** (seconds 5-15)

---

## Category 1: Negative/Warning Hooks

### Hook 1.1: "Stop optimizing"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "Stop optimizing your life."

**Visual**: Person at desk with productivity apps open. Closes laptop deliberately.

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "You've optimized thoroughly. Morning routines. Habit trackers. Second brains. And at 3 AM you still wonder: optimized for what? The question isn't 'how do I do this better?' It's 'who decided this was worth doing?'"

---

### Hook 1.2: "Your meditation app"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "Your meditation app needs you to stay incomplete."

**Visual**: Phone showing meditation streak. Hand turns phone face-down.

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "Your meditation app has one tradition. Your therapist has one. I built a system with sixteen. And two witnesses to make sure you don't get lost. Subscriptions require ongoing need. Streaks require ongoing engagement. The app's success and your independence are not aligned. What would a system look like that succeeded when you outgrew it?"

---

### Hook 1.3: "10 years of therapy"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "10 years of therapy. Same patterns."

**Visual**: Person sitting, looking at camera with recognition (not defeat).

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "I can narrate my childhood perfectly now. Explain every wound. And I'm still running the same code. Insight isn't authorship. Understanding the pattern isn't the same as writing a new one."

---

### Hook 1.4: "The course trap"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "You've bought 47 courses. Completed 3."

**Visual**: Split screen - course library vs. unchanged daily life.

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "Changed from zero. I know—I was you. Information accumulation is the most sophisticated form of avoidance. We keep consuming because generating is harder. What if you stopped learning frameworks and started examining how you build them?"

## Category 2: Curiosity/Secret Hooks

### Hook 2.1: "The pattern"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "There's a pattern connecting therapy, meditation apps, and self-help courses."

**Visual**: Three objects representing each, arranged on table. Hand draws invisible line connecting them.

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "Therapy gives narrative, not authorship. Apps teach observation, not examination of the observer. Courses deliver frameworks, not capacity to build your own. They all position you as user. Never as author. What if I told you there's a system that uses 13 ancient and modern traditions — Vedic, Western, biofield — and succeeds when you no longer need it? See the pattern?"

---

### Hook 2.2: "The upstream question"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "The question behind every question you ask yourself:"

**Visual**: Close-up of person's face, contemplative. Not dramatic.

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "'What should I do?' becomes 'Who is asking?' 'How do I feel?' becomes 'Who is feeling?' That shift—from the question to the questioner—that's where the leverage is. Everything else is downstream."

---

### Hook 2.3: "Grammar vs. tools"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "Most people learn tools. Few learn the grammar behind tools."

**Visual**: Person using a tool (writing, typing). Pause. Then examining hands.

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "You can use a meditation technique without understanding why it works. You can follow a framework without knowing how frameworks form. The difference between tool and grammar is the difference between literacy and fluency. Which are you developing?"

---

### Hook 2.4: "The exhaustion"
**Opening Line** (0-3s):
> "The exhaustion you feel isn't from seeking."

**Visual**: Person putting down a self-help book. Looking at camera.

**Script (3-15s)**:
> "It's from seeking within structures that need your dependency to survive. Every system you've tried benefits from your continued incompleteness. What would it feel like to engage with something designed to succeed when you leave?"

## Production Notes

### Filming Style
- Static camera, minimal movement
- Natural light, slightly moody
- Direct eye contact (when face shown)
- No rapid cuts, no transitions
- Clean, uncluttered backgrounds (Bone/#F5F0E8 tones)

### Audio
- Recorded voice, clear and direct
- No background music during hook
- Subtle ambient after hook (if any)
- No hype, no excitement in delivery

### Text Overlays
- Minimal, only key phrases
- Cormorant Garamond for display
- Source Sans for body
- Deep Ink (#1A1A2E) or Bone (#F5F0E8)

### Pacing
- Hook: 0-3 seconds (grab attention)
- Development: 3-12 seconds (build tension)
- Shift: 12-15 seconds (introduce the alternative)

### What NOT to Do
- No pointing at text
- No dancing or trending audio
- No "like and follow for more"
- No excessive energy
- No engagement bait
