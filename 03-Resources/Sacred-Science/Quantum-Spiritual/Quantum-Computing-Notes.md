### Definition
A **Quantum Computer** uses quantum mechanical phenomena (superposition, entanglement) to perform operations on data.
*   **Classical:** Bits (0 or 1). Physically realized by macroscopic systems (magnetization, charge).
*   **Quantum:** Qubits. Can represent and structure data using quantum properties.

### The Fundamental Difference
> "Where a classical computer obeys the well understood laws of classical physics, a quantum computer is a device that harnesses physical phenomenon unique to quantum mechanics (especially quantum interference) to realize a fundamentally new mode of information processing."

### The "Many Universes" Test
**David Deutsch** (inventor of the quantum computer concept in the 1970s) proposed it as a way to experimentally test the **"Many Universes Theory"**.
*   **Idea:** When a particle changes, it changes into all possible forms across multiple universes. A quantum computer leverages this "parallel processing" across realities.

**Stephen Hsu** draws a parallel between SETI and finding a "message from the Creator".
*   **SETI Assumption:** Aliens would send a non-random signal (like digits of Pi).
*   **Creator Hypothesis:** The Creator might hallmark the universe with a message about universal laws (e.g., simple matrices of the algebra describing fundamental forces).
*   **Implication:** We might find "very specific information that all the physicists of all the advanced civilisations would immediately recognise."

## üß¨ Evolution & Complexity (Behe)
*   **Argument:** Michael Behe argues that the odds of complex mutations (like chloroquine resistance in malaria) arising by chance are astronomically low (1 in 10^20).
*   **Counter-Argument:** Critics argue Behe calculates the odds of *specific* simultaneous mutations, not the accumulation of variations over time.
