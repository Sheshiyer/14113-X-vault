# Vedic Geometry: The Ancient Indian Science of Shapes

## Metadata

*   **Context:** Explores the four pillars of geometry in ancient India: Kalpa (Rituals), Jyotisha (Astronomy), Shilpa Shastra (Architecture), and Sri Vidya (Yantras).

## Key Concepts

### 1. Kalpa & Sulba Sutras
*   **Sulba Sutras:** "Aphorisms of the Rope." The oldest known applied geometry, used for constructing Vedic fire altars (*Yagna Kundas*).
*   **Baudhayana Theorem:** The so-called "Pythagoras Theorem" was described by Maharishi Baudhayana (~800 BC) centuries before Pythagoras.
*   **Squaring the Circle:** Geometric methods to construct a circle with the same area as a square (and vice versa) without knowing the value of Pi.
*   **Complex Altars:** The *Shyena Chiti* (Falcon Altar) consists of 1000 bricks in 5 layers (200 per layer), with alternating layouts for stability and precision.

### 2. Jyotisha (Astronomy)
*   **Time & Calculus:** The need for precise timekeeping and planetary calculations led to the development of trigonometry and early calculus.
*   **Madhava (14th Century):** Discovered the infinite series for Pi (Madhava-Gregory series) centuries before Leibniz.
*   **Aryabhata (500 CE):** Calculated the area of a circle as (Circumference/2) Ã— Radius.

### 3. Shilpa Shastra (Architecture)
*   **Temple Design:** Geometric principles guide the construction of temples, pillars, *Vimanas*, and *Gopurams*.
*   **Aesthetics & Structure:** Geometry ensures both structural integrity and aesthetic beauty in sculpture and architecture.

### 4. Sri Vidya (Yantras)
*   **Sacred Diagrams:** *Yantras* are complex geometrical manifestations of deities (e.g., Sri Yantra, Ganesha Yantra).
*   **Precision:** Construction requires extreme precision, often involving interlocking triangles (as in the Sri Yantra) representing the union of Shiva and Shakti.
