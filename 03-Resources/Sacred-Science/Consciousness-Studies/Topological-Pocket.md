## üîë Key Concepts

### 1. The Boundary Problem
*   **Fuzzy Systems:** In a universe of atoms and forces, there are no objective boundaries. "Systems" (like a banana or a brain) are just arbitrary groupings drawn by an observer.
*   **The Problem:** If consciousness is real and causally efficacious, it must have objective boundaries. "Fuzzy" computations cannot bootstrap non-fuzzy experiences.

### 2. Field Topology as Solution
*   **Frame Invariance:** Unlike spatial coordinates, **Topology** is invariant under Lorentz transformations. It doesn't change based on the observer's speed or position.
*   **Causal Significance:** Topological features have real physical effects (e.g., **Magnetic Reconnection** causing solar flares).
*   **Hypothesis:** Experiences are topological pockets of the EM field. They are "natural kinds" that "carve nature at its joints."

### 3. Implications
*   **Against Digital Sentience:** Classical computers, lacking these topological field structures, cannot be conscious. They produce only "mind dust" or are epiphenomenal.
*   **Temporal Depth:** Experiences are not instantaneous points but have temporal thickness (possibly related to 40Hz gamma oscillations).
*   **Active Inference:** These topological pockets act as "Markov Blankets," modeling their environment from the inside.
