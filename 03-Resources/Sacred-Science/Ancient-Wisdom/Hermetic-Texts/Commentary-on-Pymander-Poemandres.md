# Commentary on Pymander (Poimandres)

## Metadata
*   **Author:** G. R. S. Mead
*   **Source:** *Thrice Greatest Hermes, Vol. II*
*   **Topics:** Hermeticism, Pymander, Vision, Apocalypse, Gnosis, Corpus Hermeticum
*   **Context:** An in-depth commentary on the *Pymander* treatise, exploring its status as the "Ground Gospel" of Hermeticism and its apocalyptic nature.

## Key Points

### The Vision and Apocalypse
*   **Ground Gospel:** The *Pymander* is the most important document of the Hermetic literature, representing the "Ground Gospel" or revelation received by the founder (personified as Hermes Trismegistus).
*   **Nature of Vision:** It describes a "spiritual Epopteia" (seeing beyond) or cosmic consciousness, where the seer perceives reality in a higher octave.
*   **Interpretation:** The vision is "same" for all, but its description is conditioned by the seer's "matter" (cultural, philosophical, scientific background).

### The Corpus Hermeticum
*   **Anonymous/Pseudographic:** The literature is a grouping of personal visions and traditions, not a single author's work.
*   **Inspiration:** Written by men who believed they were under spiritual guidance to recombine the best of other scriptures into a new whole.

### The Vision of Creation
*   **The Presence:** The seer connects with a Presence (Mind to Mind) rather than just seeing.
*   **Light and Darkness:** The vision begins with Limitless Light (Mind/God). Darkness appears as a shadow, coiling like a Great Snake (Chaos/Matter), yearning for the Light.
*   **The Holy Word:** A Logos descends into the Darkness/Nature, ordering the elements (Fire, Air, Water-Earth).

### Emanations and Cosmology
*   **First Emanation (Logos):** The descent of Reason into Nature.
*   **The Pleroma:** A vision of the Fullness of God, containing the Archetypal Forms.
*   **God Desiring Himself:** The Primal Elements are the "passions of God's Will desiring Himself" (God as Mother/Wisdom desiring God as Father).
*   **Second Emanation (Mind the Maker):** The Demiurge, or Enforming Mind, which fashions the Seven Ruling Spheres (Fate) to enclose the sensible cosmos.
*   **Third Emanation (Man):** The descent of the Anthropos (Man), the Image of God, who breaks through the spheres to unite with Nature in love.

### The Mystery of Man
*   **Hermaphrodite Origins:** The first men were seven hermaphroditic types of fire and spirit, later separating into sexes.
*   **The Fall and Ascent:** Man is bound by love for Nature (body) but is essentialy free (Mind). The path of deathlessness is **Self-Knowledge**.
*   **Ascent of the Soul:** At death, the body dissolves, and the soul strips off its irrational tendencies (garment of shame) as it passes through the Seven Spheres.
*   **The Eighth Sphere (Ogdoad):** The soul enters the Ogdoad, the state of "those-that-are," and hears the Song of the Powers, eventually merging with God (Theosis).

### Thrice-Greatest Hermes
*   **Meaning:** "Thrice-Greatest" refers to the three states of consciousness or bodies (similar to the Buddhist *Trikaya*).
*   **The Shepherd:** "Pymander" is traditionally interpreted as "Shepherd of Men" (Good Shepherd), though philologically it may come from Coptic *py-meretre* ("The Witness").
