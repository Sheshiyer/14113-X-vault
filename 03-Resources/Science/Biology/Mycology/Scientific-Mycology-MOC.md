## üéØ Key Themes & Research Areas

### Fungal Ecology

**Decomposition & Nutrient Cycling**:
- Primary decomposers of lignin and cellulose
- Return nutrients from dead organisms to soil
- Carbon sequestration in soil
- Foundation of terrestrial ecosystems

**Mycorrhizal Networks** ("Wood-Wide Web"):
- 90% of plants depend on mycorrhizal partnerships
- Nutrient exchange (phosphorus, nitrogen for carbon)
- Plant-to-plant communication via mycelial networks
- Suzanne Simard's groundbreaking research
- Forest health and biodiversity maintenance

**Symbiotic Relationships**:
- Lichens (fungi + algae/cyanobacteria)
- Endophytic fungi (living within plant tissues)
- Termite agriculture (fungal gardens)
- Leaf-cutter ant cultivation

### Environmental Solutions

**Mycoremediation Applications**:
- **Heavy Metals**: Bioaccumulation in fruiting bodies
- **Petroleum**: Degradation of hydrocarbons (oil spills)
- **Pesticides**: Breakdown of agricultural chemicals
- **Nuclear Waste**: Radionuclide absorption (Chernobyl fungi)
- **Plastics**: Emerging research on plastic-eating fungi

**Stamets' Mycofiltration**:
- Water purification through mycelium mats
- Bacterial filtration (E. coli removal)
- Salmon habitat restoration
- Storm water treatment
- Agricultural runoff cleanup

**Carbon Sequestration**:
- Mycelium stores carbon in soil
- Glomalin production (soil carbon storage)
- Climate change mitigation potential
- Long-term soil carbon stability

### Biotechnology & Innovation

**Pharmaceutical Production**:
- Antibiotic discovery (Penicillium)
- Immunosuppressants (Cyclosporine from Tolypocladium)
- Cholesterol-lowering statins (Aspergillus)
- Cancer drugs (Taxol precursors)

**Industrial Enzymes**:
- Cellulases for biofuel production
- Laccases for paper bleaching
- Proteases for food processing
- Amylases for textile industry

**Sustainable Materials**:
- Mycelium leather (Mylo, Reishi leather)
- Mycelium packaging (Mushroom Packaging)
- Building materials (mycelium bricks)
- Biodegradable alternatives to plastics

## üå± Practical Applications

### DIY Mycoremediation

**Home/Community Scale**:
1. Identify pollutant (petroleum, heavy metals, pesticides)
2. Select appropriate fungal species (oyster for oil, others for metals)
3. Create mycelium mats or inoculated substrate
4. Apply to contaminated area
5. Monitor degradation over weeks/months

**Species for Remediation**:
- **Oyster mushrooms** (Pleurotus): Petroleum, pesticides
- **Turkey tail** (Trametes versicolor): E. coli, bacteria
- **Reishi** (Ganoderma): Heavy metals
- **White rot fungi**: Lignin-like pollutants

### Citizen Science Participation

**Radical Mycology Approach**:
- Document local fungi (iNaturalist)
- Test local remediation needs
- Create community cultivation spaces
- Share results open-source
- Build resilience networks
