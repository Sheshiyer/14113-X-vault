### 1. **Elemental Body Sooktha**



`ॐ भूम्यप्तेजोवायुराकाशस्वरूपाय विद्महे,   पञ्चमहाभूतात्मने धीमहि।   तन्नो मूलप्राण प्रचोदयात्॥`  

**Transliteration:** _Om Bhūmyaptejovāyurākāśasvarūpāya Vidmahe,  
Pañcamahābhūtātmane Dhīmahi.  
Tanno Mūlaprāṇa Prachodayāt._

**Translation:** "We meditate upon the essence of the five great elements—earth, water, fire, air, and ether.  
May the primal life force invigorate our elemental body."

---

### 2. **Sexual Body Sooktha**



`ॐ कामेश्वराय विद्महे,   शुद्धशक्त्यै धीमहि।   तन्नः कामप्राणः प्रचोदयात्॥`  

**Transliteration:** _Om Kāmeśvarāya Vidmahe,  
Śuddhaśaktyai Dhīmahi.  
Tannaḥ Kāmaprāṇaḥ Prachodayāt._

**Translation:** "We meditate upon the lord of desires,  
May the pure energy cleanse and invigorate our sexual body with vital force."

---

### 3. **Emotional Body Sooktha**



`ॐ रुद्राय विद्महे,   शुद्धभावाय धीमहि।   तन्नः रुद्रप्राणः प्रचोदयात्॥`  

**Transliteration:** _Om Rudrāya Vidmahe,  
Śuddhabhāvāya Dhīmahi.  
Tannaḥ Rudraprāṇaḥ Prachodayāt._

**Translation:** "We meditate upon Rudra,  
The embodiment of pure emotions,  
May the fierce life force cleanse and invigorate our emotional body."

---

### 4. **Egoic Body Sooktha**



`ॐ अहंकाराय विद्महे,   निर्मलात्मने धीमहि।   तन्नः सत्त्वप्राणः प्रचोदयात्॥`  

**Transliteration:** _Om Ahankārāya Vidmahe,  
Nirmalātmane Dhīmahi.  
Tannaḥ Sattvaprāṇaḥ Prachodayāt._

**Translation:** "We meditate upon the ego,  
The pure self within,  
May the sattvic life force cleanse and invigorate our egoic body."

---

### 5. **Casual Body Sooktha**



`ॐ करणात्मने विद्महे,   नित्यज्योतये धीमहि।   तन्नः कारणप्राणः प्रचोदयात्॥`  

**Transliteration:** _Om Karaṇātmane Vidmahe,  
Nityajyotaye Dhīmahi.  
Tannaḥ Kāraṇaprāṇaḥ Prachodayāt._

**Translation:** "We meditate upon the causal body,  
The eternal light within,  
May the causal life force cleanse and invigorate our casual body."

---

### 6. **Task Body Sooktha**



`ॐ कर्माधाराय विद्महे,   धर्मसाध्याय धीमहि।   तन्नः कर्मप्राणः प्रचोदयात्॥`  

**Transliteration:** _Om Karmādhārāya Vidmahe,  
Dharmasādhyāya Dhīmahi.  
Tannaḥ Karmaprāṇaḥ Prachodayāt._

**Translation:** "We meditate upon the foundation of our tasks,  
Aligned with righteousness,  
May the life force of action cleanse and invigorate our task body."

---

### 7. **Ancestor Body Sooktha**



`ॐ पितृदेवाय विद्महे,   सर्वज्ञात्मने धीमहि।   तन्नः पितृप्राणः प्रचोदयात्॥`  

**Transliteration:** _Om Pitṛdevāya Vidmahe,  
Sarvajñātmane Dhīmahi.  
Tannaḥ Pitṛprāṇaḥ Prachodayāt._

**Translation:** "We meditate upon the ancestral spirits,  
The all-knowing soul,  
May the ancestral life force cleanse and invigorate our ancestor body."


The **Purusha Sooktha** from the **Mahanarayana Upanishad** is one of the most revered hymns in the Vedic tradition. It glorifies the cosmic being or **Purusha**, the universal consciousness and essence pervading all existence. Here's a translation of the key verses from the **Mahanarayana Upanishad** version of the **Purusha Sooktha**:

---

### **Purusha Sooktha Translation**

**1.**
Sahasraśīrṣā puruṣaḥ | sahasrākṣaḥ sahasrapāt |  
Sa bhūmiṃ viśvato vṛtvā | atyatiṣṭhaddaśāṅgulam || 1 ||

The Purusha (Cosmic Being) has a thousand heads, a thousand eyes, and a thousand feet.  
He pervades the Earth in all directions and transcends it by ten fingers' breadth.

---

**2.**
Puruṣa evedaṃ sarvam | yad bhūtaṃ yacca bhavyam |  
Utāmṛtatvasyeśāno | yadannenātirohati || 2 ||

This Purusha is all that has been, all that is, and all that will be.  
He is the Lord of immortality, who grows beyond through food.

---

**3.**
Etāvānasya mahimā | ato jyāyāṃśca pūruṣaḥ |  
Pādo'sya viśvā bhūtāni | tripādasyāmṛtaṃ divi || 3 ||

Such is the greatness of this Purusha. Greater indeed is He than all.  
All beings constitute but one-quarter of Him; the other three-quarters are immortal, beyond the earthly realm.

---

**4.**
Tripādūrdhva udaitpuruṣaḥ | pādo'syehābhavāt punaḥ |  
Tato viṣvaṅ vyakrāmat | sāśanānaśane abhi || 4 ||

The three-quarters of the Purusha rose upwards, and His one-quarter remained here.  
From that, He spread out in all directions, pervading both the living and the non-living.

---

**5.**
Tasmād virāḍ ajāyata | virājo adhi pūruṣaḥ |  
Sajāto atyaricyata | paścād bhūmimatho puraḥ || 5 ||

From that Purusha, Virat (the cosmic form) was born;  
And from Virat, the Purusha was born again,  
Once born, He extended beyond the Earth,  
both in front and behind.

---

**6.**
Yat puruṣeṇa haviṣā | devā yajñamatanvata |  
Vasanto asyāsīdājyaṃ | grīṣma idhmaḥ śarad dhaviḥ || 6 ||

When the gods performed a sacrifice with the Purusha as the oblation,  
spring was the clarified butter, summer was the fuel, and autumn was the oblation.

---

**7.**
Saptāsyaāsan paridhayaḥ | triḥ sapta samidhaḥ kṛtāḥ |  
Devā yad yajñaṃ tanvānāḥ | abadhnan puruṣaṃ paśum || 7 ||

Seven were the enclosing sticks, and thrice seven were the sacrificial fuels.  
The gods bound the Purusha as the sacrificial offering.

---

**8.**
Yajñena yajñam ayajanta devāḥ | tāni dharmāṇi prathamānyāsan |  
Te ha nākaṃ mahimānaḥ sacante | yatra pūrve sādhyāḥ santi devāḥ || 8 ||

With the sacrifice, the gods performed the sacrifice.  
These were the first principles of Dharma (righteousness).  
Those mighty ones now dwell in the supreme heaven, where the ancient Sādhyas (gods) reside.

---

**9.**
Tasmād yajñāt sarvahutaḥ | sambhṛtaṃ pṛṣadājyam |  
Paśūn tāṃścakre vāyavyān | āraṇyān grāmyāśca ye || 9 ||

From that sacrifice, where the entire offering was made,  
curds and clarified butter were produced.  
He created animals, both wild and domesticated.

---

**10.**
Tasmād yajñāt sarvahutaḥ | ṛcaḥ sāmāni jajñire |  
Chandāṃsi jajñire tasmāt | yajus tasmād ajāyata || 10 ||

From that sacrifice, where everything was offered,  
the Rig Veda and Sama Veda were born.  
The meters were born from it, and the Yajur Veda was born from it.

---

**11.**
Tasmād aśvājāyanta | ye ke cobhayādataḥ |  
Gāvo ha jajñire tasmāt | tasmāj jāta ajāvayaḥ || 11 ||

From it, horses were born, and all animals with two sets of teeth.  
Cows were born from it, and goats and sheep were born from it.

---

**12.**
Yat puruṣaṃ vyadadhuḥ | katidhā vyakalpayan |  
Mukhaṃ kim asya kau bāhū | kā ūrū pādā ucyete || 12 ||

When they divided the Purusha, into how many parts did they distribute Him?  
What was His mouth? What were His arms? What were called His thighs and feet?

---

**13.**
Brāhmaṇo'sya mukham āsīt | bāhū rājanyaḥ kṛtaḥ |  
Ūrū tad asya yad vaiśyaḥ | padbhyāṃ śūdro ajāyata || 13 ||

The Brahmin was His mouth, the Kshatriya was made His arms,  
The Vaishya was His thighs, and from His feet, the Shudra was born.

---

**14.**
Candramā manaso jātaḥ | cakṣoḥ sūryo ajāyata |  
Mukhad indrāgniś ca | prāṇād vāyur ajāyata || 14 ||

The moon was born from His mind; the sun was born from His eyes.  
Indra and Agni came from His mouth, and from His breath, the wind was born.

---

**15.**
Nābhyā āsīd antarikṣaṃ | śīrṣṇo dyauḥ samavartata |  
Padbhyāṃ bhūmir diśaḥ śrotrāt | tathā lokāṃ akalpayan || 15 ||

From His navel arose the sky, from His head, the heavens emerged,  
From His feet, the Earth, and from His ears, the directions.  
Thus the worlds were fashioned.

---

**16.**
Vedāham etam puruṣaṃ mahāntam | ādityavarṇaṃ tamasaḥ parastāt |  
Tamevaṃ vidvān amṛta iha bhavati | nānyaḥ panthā vidyate'yanāya || 16 ||

I know that great Purusha, resplendent like the sun, beyond all darkness.  
Knowing Him alone, one attains immortality.  
There is no other path for liberation.

---

**17.**
Prajāpatis carati garbhe antaḥ | ajāyamāno bahudhā vijāyate |  
Tasya dhīrāḥ parijānanti yonim | mariçyānām padamicchanti vedhasaḥ || 17 ||

Prajapati moves within the womb, unborn yet appearing in manifold forms.  
The wise discern His presence and seek the path of those who know the eternal truth.

---

The concept of **Osiris** in Egyptian mythology is traditionally associated with the afterlife, but if we interpret the myth metaphorically, the "afterlife" Osiris presides over can also be seen as representing **rebirth, renewal, and the process of life itself**. In this framework, the afterlife is not just about what happens after physical death but also about **cyclical renewal**, including the **birth from the womb**, which mirrors the themes of **rebirth** and **regeneration** seen throughout nature and human life.

### **Osiris and the Afterlife (Rebirth from the Womb)**

In the myth of Osiris:

- **Osiris is killed and dismembered** by his brother Seth, symbolizing destruction and the disintegration of order.
- His wife **Isis** collects the scattered pieces of his body and, using her magic, **reassembles him**, bringing him back to life, albeit in the afterworld.
- This resurrection of Osiris symbolizes the **eternal cycle of life, death, and rebirth**, much like the natural world’s cycle of decay and renewal, and the human cycle of **birth** and **death**.

If we reframe the **afterlife** as not just the life after death but also **the continuation of life itself**, then Osiris can represent the **process of gestation and birth**:
- Just as Osiris is reborn in the afterlife, human life begins after gestation in the **womb** and emerges into the physical world.
- The **womb** can be metaphorically understood as a transitional space (similar to the afterlife) where the soul or essence waits before **rebirth** into the physical world, just as Osiris is resurrected into a new form.

This cyclical interpretation suggests that the process of **creation, destruction, and rebirth**—whether in the cosmic, agricultural, or human life—is central to the myth of Osiris. In this light, the **afterlife** could symbolize the **period of gestation** before **birth**, where the soul prepares for entry into the physical world, much as Osiris is prepared for resurrection and rulership of the underworld.

### **10 Similarities between the Purusha Sooktha and the Body of Osiris**

1. **Creation through Sacrifice/Disintegration**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: The universe is created by the sacrifice of Purusha, whose body parts form different aspects of the cosmos.
   - **Body of Osiris**: Osiris’s dismemberment leads to the creation of new life and symbolizes the ongoing cycle of birth and renewal.

2. **Symbolic Dismemberment of the Divine Body**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: Purusha’s body is metaphorically divided into various components of creation, such as the sun, moon, and social orders.
   - **Body of Osiris**: Osiris’s physical body is literally dismembered, and the scattered parts represent the cycle of life, death, and rebirth, reflecting natural processes.

3. **Restoration and Rebirth**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: Through the cosmic sacrifice, the body of Purusha is eternally present, representing the immortality of the universe.
   - **Body of Osiris**: After being reassembled by Isis, Osiris is reborn in the afterlife, representing the continuation of life through renewal.

4. **Association with Fertility and Life Cycles**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: The sacrifice of Purusha generates all life and the elements, establishing order in the cosmos.
   - **Body of Osiris**: Osiris is closely tied to the Nile and the fertility of the land. His resurrection represents the agricultural cycle of planting, growth, and harvest, analogous to life emerging from death.

5. **Governance of the Cosmic Order**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: The Purusha embodies the universe, governing cosmic law (Rta) through His body.
   - **Body of Osiris**: Osiris governs the moral order of the afterlife, ensuring justice and balance in the next life, which reflects the cosmic order.

6. **Dual Realms (Material and Spiritual)**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: The Purusha exists in both the material world (His one-quarter) and the spiritual world (His three-quarters), representing both dimensions of existence.
   - **Body of Osiris**: Osiris’s role as a god in the afterlife bridges the material (earthly) existence and the spiritual (afterworld), linking both realms.

7. **Embodiment of Cycles**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: The creation and dissolution of the universe through Purusha’s body represents the eternal cycle of time (Samsara).
   - **Body of Osiris**: Osiris embodies the cycles of nature—death and rebirth—as seen in the Nile’s flooding, which brings fertility to the land.

8. **Connection with the Elements**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: Purusha’s body is associated with natural elements, including the sun, moon, earth, and waters, which are integral to creation.
   - **Body of Osiris**: Osiris’s myth is closely linked to water (the Nile), earth (fertility), and the cycles of nature, symbolizing elemental connections.

9. **The Role of the Divine Feminine**:
   - **Purusha Sooktha**: While not explicitly mentioned in this hymn, Vedic cosmology often includes feminine aspects of creation (Prakriti, Shakti) that enable the sacrifice to unfold.
   - **Body of Osiris**: Isis, the divine feminine, plays a central role in the resurrection of Osiris, gathering his body and restoring him, symbolizing the creative power of the feminine.

10. **Cosmic Oversight of Life and Death**:
    - **Purusha Sooktha**: The Purusha’s presence ensures the ongoing process of creation and destruction, life and death.
    - **Body of Osiris**: As the god of the afterlife, Osiris oversees the passage of souls, guiding the dead through their resurrection, much like how life continually moves through cycles of existence.

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Purusha Sooktha** and the **myth of Osiris** share profound symbolic similarities, though each myth belongs to a different cultural framework. Both depict a **divine body** that undergoes transformation—through sacrifice or dismemberment—leading to the creation and regeneration of life. They both highlight the importance of **cycles** in the universe: the constant interplay between creation, destruction, death, and rebirth. In Osiris’s case, the concept of rebirth can be expanded to include **birth from the womb**, reinforcing his association with the regeneration of life, while Purusha’s body reflects the grand, universal process of creation itself. Both traditions thus affirm the eternal continuity of existence, whether in the cosmos or in human life.
