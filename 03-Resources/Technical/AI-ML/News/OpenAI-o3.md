Adobe Research unveils Sketch2Sound to create sound effects with your voice
(Image Source: Hugofloresgarcia)
Adobe and Northwestern University recently unveiled
Sketch2Sound
, an AI tool that transforms vocal imitations and text descriptions into professional sound effects and atmospheres.
Here's what you need to know:
Users can generate professional audio by humming, mimicking sounds, or describing their desired effects in simple text.
The tool analyzes volume, timbre, and pitch from vocal inputs, combining them with text descriptions to create precise sound effects.
Users can also fine-tune control with built-in filtering technology, offering either detailed or approximate sound generation based on their needs.
Adobe has not disclosed a release timeline or whether Sketch2Sound will become a commercial product.
Why it matters:
Sketch2Sound has the potential to revolutionize sound design, offering a faster and more flexible way to create sound effects. This tool can be especially valuable for sound designers and Foley artists, simplifying their process and allowing for quick experimentation without the need for physical props.
