In 1950, for his 75th birthday, **C.G. Jung** set up a stone cube on the lakeshore near his tower at Bollingen. He inscribed three sides with texts reflecting his alchemical and psychological insights.

## ü™® The Inscriptions

### Side 1: The Alchemical Stone
*   **Latin Quote:** *Rosarium philosophorum*
*   **Text:** "hic lapis exilis extat, pretio quoque vilis, spernitur a stultis, amatur plus ab edoctis"
*   **Translation:** "This stone is poor, and cheap in price; it is disdained by fools, but it is loved all the more by the wise."
*   **Dedication:** "IN MEMORIAM NAT[ivitati]S DIEI LXXV C G JUNG EX GRAT[itudine] FEC[it] ET POS[uit] A[nn]O MCML" (In memory of his 75th birthday, C.G. Jung out of gratitude made and set up [this stone], in the year 1950).

### Side 2: Telesphorus
*   **Figure:** Telesphorus (a dwarf/homunculus with a lantern and hooded cape).
*   **Greek Inscription:**
    *   "Time is a child at play, gambling; a child's is the kingship." (Heraclitus)
    *   "Telesphorus traverses the dark places of the world, like a star flashing from the deep, leading the way to the Gates of the Sun and the Land of Dreams." (Reference to Odyssey 24.11).

### Side 3: The Mandala
*   **Central Figure:** Homunculus-Mercurius-Telesphorus.
*   **Structure:** A quaternary Mandala of alchemical significance:
    *   **Top:** Saturn
    *   **Bottom:** Mars
    *   **Left:** Sol-Jupiter ("male")
    *   **Right:** Luna-Venus ("female")
