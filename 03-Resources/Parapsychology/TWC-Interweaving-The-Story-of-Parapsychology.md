# Interweaving; The Story of Parapsychology

## Summary

A comprehensive historical survey of parapsychological research from premodern accounts through the founding of the Society for Psychical Research to contemporary laboratory studies. The video examines the evidence for telepathy, clairvoyance, remote viewing, psychokinesis, and related phenomena, while also documenting the systematic institutional opposition that has shaped public perception of the field. It argues that the popular dismissal of parapsychology as pseudoscience is itself a purposefully engineered misconception, driven by both intentional manipulation of academic opinion and unintentional biases inherent in materialist philosophical assumptions.

## Key Insights

1. **Institutional engineering of skepticism**: The popular perception of parapsychology as pseudoscience has been as much the result of intentional engineering of academic opinion as of unintentional philosophical biases. The history reveals coordinated efforts to discredit legitimate research.

2. **Premodern precedents**: Accounts of telepathy, clairvoyance, and related phenomena extend throughout recorded human history across virtually all cultures, predating and transcending the modern scientific framework within which they are typically evaluated.

3. **Rigorous methodology**: Contrary to popular belief, the best parapsychological research has employed methodological standards that are often more rigorous than those found in mainstream psychology, including pre-registration, double-blinding, and statistical controls that exceed standard practice.

4. **Freud's secret sympathy**: Even Freud acknowledged the objective possibility of thought transference and telepathy, though he was reluctant to publicize this for fear of damaging psychoanalysis's scientific credibility -- a pattern repeated across many major thinkers.

5. **Philosophical implications**: The evidence from parapsychology, if taken seriously, has profound implications for our understanding of consciousness, time, causality, and the nature of reality -- connecting directly to questions in quantum mechanics and process philosophy.

## Themes & Patterns

- **Science vs. scientism**: The distinction between genuine scientific inquiry (which follows evidence wherever it leads) and scientism (which prejudges what evidence is acceptable based on metaphysical commitments).
- **The replication crisis in context**: Parapsychological effects, while small, replicate at rates comparable to or better than many accepted findings in mainstream psychology.
- **Cross-cultural universality**: The consistency of reported psi phenomena across cultures and historical periods suggests something more than culturally specific folklore or psychological artifacts.
- **The "forbidden" character of psi**: The intense institutional resistance to psi research itself becomes a datum requiring explanation -- why does this particular domain of inquiry provoke such disproportionate hostility?
